Mission: To create inclusive, accessible, and meaningful employment opportunities for individuals facing economic, physical, or social barriers, promoting dignity, independence, and long-term community stability.

Executive Summary

This initiative seeks grant funding to support a community-based employment program serving individuals in need of fair and flexible work opportunities. The program is inclusive of deaf and disabled workers, veterans, young adults, older individuals, and anyone experiencing financial hardship.

Who This Program Serves

  • Deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals
  • People with physical, cognitive, or developmental disabilities
  • Military veterans transitioning to civilian employment
  • Young adults entering the workforce
  • Older adults seeking continued or renewed employment
  • Anyone experiencing financial hardship or barriers to work

Expected Impact

  • Increased employment access for underserved populations
  • Reduced financial stress for individuals and families
  • Improved confidence, skills, and workforce participation
  • Stronger community inclusion and economic stability

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply?
Anyone in need of employment support may apply. This includes disabled individuals, veterans, young adults, older individuals, and people experiencing financial hardship.

Is this program part-time or full-time?
Both part-time and full-time opportunities are available depending on individual needs and capacity.

Do I need prior experience?
No. The program focuses on accessibility, flexibility, and skill development.

Is there an age limit?
No. The program is open to adults of all ages.

How will grant funds be used?
Funds will support inclusive hiring, workplace accommodations, outreach, training, and long-term program sustainability.
